# Project Galáctica

> **A Hard Sci-Fi Grand Strategy Game of Biochemistry, Relativity, and Survival.**

## Overview
*Project Galáctica* is a real-time grand strategy game set entirely within a localized stellar cluster. Inspired by the hard science fiction of *Project Hail Mary* and the deep empire-building of *Stellaris*, this project strips away the "space magic" of traditional sci-fi. There are no magical warp drives, no instantaneous universal communicators, and no Earth-centric biases about what constitutes a habitable world.

Every mechanic in *Project Galáctica* is grounded in real-world astrophysics, relativistic physics, and astrobiology. You must manage your empire while fighting against the brutal realities of the speed of light, planetary biochemistry, and the fact that you, the player, are a physical, killable entity on the board.

## Map Scale & MVP Scope
To ensure a tight, highly tactical gameplay loop, the game map is restricted to a **10-light-year radius**. 
* **Realistic Star Charting:** The map is populated with real, historically mapped star systems (e.g., Earth in the Sol system) and planets from our localized stellar cluster.
* **Static MVP Map:** While the positioning is realistic, the celestial mechanics are not simulated. For the MVP, the galactic map is entirely static; planets and star systems do not orbit.
* **Claustrophobic Play Area:** This restricts play to roughly a dozen star systems, ensuring that every resource, planet, and travel route is fiercely contested.
* **Endless Survival:** There is no traditional "Win State" or victory condition. The ultimate goal is indefinite survival, expansion, and management of your civilization against the lethal realities of the localized cluster.
* By universally capping ship speeds at non-relativistic limits (e.g., 0.1c), the MVP bypasses mathematically frustrating time-dilation mechanics. This ensures strategy remains laser-focused on the incredibly high tension of FTL logistics and light-speed "Fog of War" communications.

## Core Pillars & Key Features

### 1. Alternative Biochemistry ("Weird Life")
Habitable worlds are not restricted to liquid water. The galaxy is populated by lifeforms utilizing alternative biological solvents. To expand your empire, you must research and adapt to these extreme biomes:
* **Ammonia ($NH_3$):** Frigid, high-efficiency cryo-worlds.
* **Methane ($CH_4$):** Ultra-cold, slow-time hydrocarbon lakes.
* **Sulfuric Acid ($H_2SO_4$):** Boiling, high-pressure Venusian hellscapes.
* **Formamide ($CH_3NO$):** Scorched, resilient desert planets.

### 2. The Wormhole Network (Galactic Terrain)
To bypass the apocalyptic time dilation of near-lightspeed travel, FTL travel relies entirely on naturally occurring Einstein-Rosen bridges. These wormholes come in five distinct size classes, acting as the galaxy's "geography." 
* Heavy dreadnoughts cannot pursue scouts through narrow-bore wormholes.
* Empires must physically secure and stabilize Class IV and V transit hubs with Exotic Matter to move massive armadas.
* Late-game tech allows for the creation of Class I "Micro-Fractures" to establish emergency data lines.

### 3. Realistic FTL Communication & The True Fog of War
Faster-Than-Light communication is impossible without breaking causality. Therefore, in *Project Galáctica*, all FTL data is transmitted via lasers shot through the physical wormhole network. 
* If a fleet jumps into an unconnected star system, they are entirely cut off from the empire.
* Destroying an enemy's wormhole toll booth doesn't just cut their supply lines; it physically blinds them to that sector of space. 

### 4. The Player Entity (The "King" Piece)
You do not play as an omnipresent god. You are the Leader of your empire, existing physically on a planet, a space station, or a flagship. 
* **Risk vs. Reward:** Stationing yourself on a central wormhole hub grants massive logistical control and perfect communication, but exposes you to decapitation strikes. Leading a fleet personally grants massive combat bonuses but risks isolating you behind enemy lines.
* **Survival:** If your flagship or station is destroyed, you must manually pilot an Escape Pod through the debris to safety. If the Leader is captured or killed, the campaign ends. 

## Development Status
*Project Galáctica* is currently in the **Pre-Production & MVP Planning** phase. The primary focus is establishing a mathematically and scientifically sound design document before engine prototyping begins.
