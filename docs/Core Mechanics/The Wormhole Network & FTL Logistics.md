# The Wormhole Network & FTL Logistics

### 1. The Science of the Network (Einstein-Rosen Bridges)
Faster-Than-Light (FTL) travel and communication in *Project Galáctica* are strictly governed by the Wormhole Network. These spacetime tunnels bypass relativistic time dilation. 

*Note: For the purposes of the MVP, the network of Einstein-Rosen bridges is **static, deterministically generated at the start of the game, and inherently stable.** They do not require active maintenance or threading with Exotic Matter to prevent collapse.*

*Note: ALL wormholes, regardless of size, allow for FTL communication via data-lasers. The size only restricts physical mass.*

### 2. Wormhole Classifications (The 5 Tiers)
Wormholes are categorized by the aperture of their throat, dictating what class of physical mass can safely pass through.

#### **Class I: Micro-Fractures (Data & Comms Only)**
* **Capacity:** Photons and energy only (Data-lasers). No physical mass.
* **Strategic Value:** The ultimate spy-glass and command relay. A system with only a Class I wormhole cannot be physically reinforced, but it grants perfect real-time vision and command capabilities. 
* **Late-Game Tech:** While naturally occurring, late-game tech allows players to artificially generate Class I wormholes. This allows a cut-off Leader on a Flagship to establish a localized "pinprick" connection back to the empire without needing a physical Space Station hub.

#### **Class II: Narrow-Bore (Small Craft & Escape Pods)**
* **Capacity:** Micro-vessels, scout ships, couriers, and Escape Pods.
* **Strategic Value:** Highly tactical "back alleys" of space. Heavy fleets cannot use them. These are crucial for stealth incursions, rapid scouting, or desperate escapes if a Leader's Flagship is destroyed in an adjacent system. Blockading these requires specialized light-interceptor patrols.

#### **Class III: Standard Gate (Cruisers & Mid-Tier Fleets)**
* **Capacity:** Standard military vessels (Frigates, Cruisers) and medium resource freighters. Ships must pass through in single-file or small formations.
* **Strategic Value:** The standard lanes of the galactic highway. These form the backbone of your empire's expansion and trade routes. Defending these is the baseline for border security.

#### **Class IV: Heavy Bridge (Capital Ships & Dreadnoughts)**
* **Capacity:** Massive capital ships, Dreadnoughts, Super-Carriers, and heavy industrial colony ships.
* **Strategic Value:** Rare and highly contested chokepoints. If an enemy empire wants to invade your core systems with their heaviest world-killer fleets, they *must* capture these specific nodes. Heavily fortifying Class IV nodes is mandatory for late-game survival.

#### **Class V: Arterial Hubs (Mass Fleet Transit)**
* **Capacity:** Entire armadas simultaneously. No bottlenecking.
* **Strategic Value:** Exceptionally rare, ancient, and massive tears in spacetime. Controlling an Arterial Hub allows a player to instantly project overwhelming, empire-ending military force across the galaxy without the standard single-file vulnerability of jumping through smaller gates. 

### 3. Artificial Wormhole Generation (Player Mechanics)
Early and mid-game expansion relies entirely on discovering and securing naturally occurring wormholes. 

* **Late-Game Tech - The "Comms Punch":** Once a player unlocks advanced physics, they can spend massive amounts of energy and Exotic Matter to artificially generate **Class I (Micro-Fracture)** wormholes. 
* **Mechanic Limit:** Players cannot artificially create Class II through V wormholes (the energy required would ignite a star). This ensures the galactic map's geography remains static and chokepoints never lose their strategic value, while still giving the player late-game logistical flexibility for information warfare.
