# Factions & Diplomacy

### 1. Truly Alien Factions (and Humanity)
While players have the option of starting as a **Human faction** originating from Earth and our realistic Sol system, the rest of the galaxy is entirely xenobiological. Aside from humanity, all other empires consist of truly alien organisms governed by alternative biochemistries. The galaxy is not filled with humanoid stand-ins; it is populated by bizarre life forms thriving in ammonia, methane, sulfuric acid, and other extreme environments. 

### 2. Pre-FTL Civilizations
Not all species encountered will be space-faring. Some alien populations have not yet unlocked space travel and remain confined to their home worlds. These primitive or developing species present unique opportunities (and ethical dilemmas) for an expanding empire.

### 3. Linguistics & Communication
Because every species is fundamentally alien, there is no universal translator or innate diplomatic channel. 
* Upon encountering a new species, **there is zero communication possible by default.** 
* To interact, empires must proactively research **Linguistics** and dedicate resources to translating the alien language. 
* Until a translation breakthrough is achieved, you cannot negotiate, trade, or form treaties with the encountered faction. They remain a completely unknown variable.

### 4. Mergers & Multiracial Organizations
Once communication is established, diplomacy opens up significantly. Factions are not strictly bound to remaining isolated species-states. Empires have the ability to diplomatically or culturally merge with other factions. This means that late-game entities can be massive, multiracial galactic organizations or multi-species "Empires," requiring players to balance the needs of drastically different xenobiological populations within a single political entity.

### 5. The Spectrum of Interaction & First Contact
*Project Galactica* provides players and AI with a massive spectrum of interaction options. Empires can adopt vastly different ethical approaches, each coming with its own unique strategic perks and drawbacks:

* **Aggression & Exploitation (The Hostile Path):** Factions can lean heavily into conquest. They can aggressively abduct and test/vivisect alien species to forcibly shortcut linguistic or biological research. They can pillage planets, kidnap populaces as forced labor, enslave entire civilizations, or systematically genocide species (or pre-FTL primitives) to clear a planet for colonization.
* **Diplomacy & Cooperation (The Benevolent Path):** Conversely, empires can prioritize a peaceful, cooperative stance. Instead of abducting primitives, they can send peaceful science teams to carefully initiate First Contact. Friendly factions can uplift primitive civilizations, establish protective protectorates, recruit diverse populations, and build robust trade networks. 
* You have the full freedom to be the galaxy's most brutal terror or the orchestrator of a utopian, multi-species federation.
